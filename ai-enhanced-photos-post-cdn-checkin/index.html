AI Enhanced Photos — post-CDN restart check-in

Flag ai_enhanced_photo_quality_0526 · window May 28 → Jun 8 PT (~11 days) · updated 2026-06-09 · PREVIEW — not yet published

Now that the enhanced photos serve correctly through the CDN, the treatment shows no detectable lift: conversion -0.83% and GMV per user -0.99%, neither significant. This reverses the +2.7% directional read from the earlier, invalid (non-CDN) run — and holds on a contamination-free new-user cohort. The funnel explains why: the enhanced photos pull more users into item-detail pages but convert fewer of them to cart, so the gains cancel. This is a directional mid-experiment read; the keep/kill call belongs to the experiment console's Bayesian view.

The enhanced photos draw more taps into item-detail pages (menu item viewed +0.78%) but convert fewer of those viewers to cart (add to cart given item viewed −1.73%, the largest single signal here). The two roughly cancel, so orders land flat. This is the design's expectation-mismatch risk in the data: the photo over-promises in the list, earns the click, then under-delivers on the detail page. Downstream of the cart, behavior is identical (~84% reach checkout in both arms). Directional mid-experiment signal, uncorrected for multiple steps.

07 · Method & assumptions

The first run (launched May 13) was invalidated because treatment photos did not serve through a CDN. Eng rewrote item photos to CDN-backed URLs on May 27 and restarted on the same flag ~5pm PT. Because the flag is reused, the window starts May 28 PT to drop all pre-CDN exposures; assignments are deduped to first exposure within the window (0 in-window flips). Primary metrics join exposed users to post-exposure orders; significance shown as Bayesian chance treatment beats control. Channel cut is session-grain on the bot-filtered cohort.
